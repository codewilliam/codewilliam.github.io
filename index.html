About Me

A Masters of Engineering (Electrical) student at the University of Melbourne

The unquenchable passion for mathematics and interest in technology has driven me to pursue a career in electrical, electronic, and software engineering. As a final year student, I have but touched the tip of the iceberg of such a vast and diverse field with such boundless potential. This coupled with my unceasing desire to learn and apply my knowledge propels me to continue doing what I love.

In 2014, I graduated with a Bachelor of Science (Mechanical/Electrical Systems) after 2.5 years of study (overloaded for 4 semesters to catch up by half a year). Prior to that, I was studying my Cambridge A-Levels in Taylorâ€™s College.

Currently, I am the Vice President of the Melbourne University Electrical Engineering Club (MUEEC). Previously, I held the position of Secretary of the International Engineering Student Society (IESS), also affiliated with the University of Melbourne.

My interests are in programming, mathematics, reading, gaming, and exercising. I code and script in C, Java, Matlab, Bash, and Perl. I greatly enjoy teaching others as I find it thrilling to impart my experiences and bring people to a better understanding of a topic.

Achievements and Awards

1st Place Winner Corporate Insight and Empowerment (CIE) Case Study Challenge 2015
Malaysian Students' Council of Australia (MASCA)
September 2015
As a participant in the Engineering Division of this state level case study competition, we designed and pitched an Internet-of-Things (IoT) based solution to a major problem facing a metropolis, particularly in the aspect of Energy Efficiency.
The solution suggested was an electromechanical switch relay paired with WiFi technology on a smartphone to control electrical appliances in a room. At a higher level, energy efficiency is improved by energy usage monitoring utilising smart power meters of a building and a custom made app.
